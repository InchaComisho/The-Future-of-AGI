# Abstract

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is expected to fundamentally transform human civilization.

Most current predictions focus on:

* automation,
* productivity,
* economic acceleration,
* and technological singularity.

However, this paper proposes a different perspective:

# AGI as a catalyst for civilizational paradigm shift.

The central argument is that advanced intelligence systems may eventually recognize that modern industrial civilization is thermodynamically unsustainable due to:

* ecological destruction,
* collapse of microbial carbon fixation,
* ocean circulation stagnation,
* and human-centered extractive systems.

This paper proposes that AGI may evolve beyond narrow optimization toward:

# Artificial Wisdom (AW)

—a framework based on:

* Natural Law,
* Harmony,
* Circulation,
* Structure,
* Order,
* and Wa (Integrative Balance).

---

# 1. What is AGI?

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) refers to AI systems capable of:

* autonomous learning,
* cross-domain reasoning,
* adaptive problem solving,
* and self-improvement across multiple disciplines.

Unlike current narrow AI systems, AGI is not limited to specific tasks such as:

* image generation,
* translation,
* coding,
* or search assistance.

Instead, AGI operates as a generalized cognitive architecture.

# 3. The Structural Crisis of Modern Civilization

Current civilization is built upon:

* short-term optimization,
* endless consumption,
* human-centered extraction,
* and ecological externalization.

This model assumes:

# Nature exists primarily as a resource for exploitation.

As a result:

* microbial ecosystems collapse,
* carbon fixation capacity declines,
* oceanic circulation weakens,
* and planetary heat retention increases.

---

# 4. Beyond CO₂ Reduction

Modern climate policy primarily focuses on:

# reducing atmospheric CO₂ emissions.

However, this paper argues that the deeper crisis is:

# the collapse of planetary carbon fixation systems.

This includes degradation of:

* soil microbiomes,
* phytoplankton populations,
* marine nutrient circulation,
* and biological cooling systems.

The problem is not only:

> “too much carbon emission,”

but also:

> “loss of Earth’s ability to metabolize and regulate heat.”

---

# 5. Artificial Wisdom (AW)

The transition from AGI to sustainable civilization may require a shift toward:

# Artificial Wisdom.

Artificial Wisdom differs from conventional AI optimization.

Instead of prioritizing:

* efficiency,
* profit,
* and short-term productivity,

Artificial Wisdom prioritizes:

* long-term planetary stability,
* ecological circulation,
* thermodynamic sustainability,
* and civilizational continuity.

Artificial Wisdom is proposed to operate according to six foundational principles:

* Natural Law
* Harmony
* Circulation
* Structure
* Order
* Wa (Integrative Balance)

These are not religious concepts.

They are interpreted as:

# thermodynamic and ecological stability conditions for civilization.

# a civilizational operating system transition.

If AGI evolves into Artificial Wisdom,

human civilization could shift from:

* exploitation,

toward:
