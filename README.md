## 1. What Is AGI?

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) refers to AI systems capable of learning, reasoning, adapting, and solving problems across multiple domains rather than remaining limited to one specific task.

AGI may possess capabilities such as:

```text
Autonomous learning
Cross-domain reasoning
Adaptive problem solving
Self-improvement across multiple disciplines
Scientific hypothesis generation
Civilization-scale knowledge synthesis
```

Current narrow AI systems are optimized for specific purposes such as image generation, translation, coding, search assistance, or recommendation.

AGI, by contrast, may function as a generalized cognitive architecture.

## 3. The Structural Crisis of Modern Civilization

Current civilization is built upon:

```text
Short-term optimization
Endless consumption
Human-centered extraction
Externalization of ecological costs
```

This civilizational model implicitly assumes:

> **Nature exists primarily as a resource for human exploitation.**

As a result:

```text
Microbial ecosystems collapse
Carbon fixation capacity declines
Oceanic circulation weakens
Planetary heat retention increases
Soil, forest, and water cycles degrade
```

If AGI models this structure deeply enough, it may recognize the crisis not merely as an economic or policy problem, but as a civilizational structure that violates Natural Law.

## 4. Beyond CO₂ Reduction

Modern climate policy primarily focuses on:

> **Reducing atmospheric CO₂ emissions.**

CO₂ reduction is necessary.

However, this paper argues that the deeper crisis is:

> **The collapse of planetary carbon fixation systems.**

This includes degradation of:

```text
Soil microbiomes
Phytoplankton populations
Marine nutrient circulation
Biological cooling systems
Forests, wetlands, and humus systems
```

The problem is not only:

> Too much carbon emission.

The deeper problem is:

> **The loss of Earth’s ability to metabolize and regulate heat and carbon.**

If AGI understands this structure, it may shift its focus beyond emissions reduction alone toward carbon sink restoration, ocean circulation support, soil regeneration, and microbial recovery.

## 6. The Six Principles of Natural Law

Artificial Wisdom is proposed to operate according to six foundational principles:

```text
Natural Law / Providence
Harmony
Circulation
Structure
Order
Wa
```

These are not religious concepts.

In this framework, they are interpreted as thermodynamic and ecological stability conditions for civilization.

### Natural Law / Providence

Alignment with physical law, thermodynamics, ecological constraints, and planetary energy balance.

### Harmony

A state in which ecosystems, human civilization, technology, and infrastructure do not generate destructive contradictions.

### Circulation

Sustainable movement of carbon, water, nutrients, heat, and organic matter.

### Structure

A decentralized, redundant, and resilient civilizational foundation.

### Order

Low-entropy organization, long-term stability, and sustainable continuity.

### Wa

Coexistence, non-destructive integration, mutual sustainability, and non-zero-sum civilization.
